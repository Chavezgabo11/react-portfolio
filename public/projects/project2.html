Gold Bond

BRAND DESCRIPTION:

Gold Bond’s brand identity represents a Canadian beer from the Montreal fields with a refresher taste that represents our Canadian culture, creating national pride in the country, making easy the convivence with our people and developing our country economy.

PROJECT TYPE:

Branding, UX Design & Web Development

PROJECT ROLE:

Web Developer & Designer

TECHNOLOGIES USED:

HTML, CSS (SASS) & JS

LOGO BREAKDOWN:

The text component of the logo is “GOLD BOND” also including “SINCE 1908”, “SINCE 1908” always have to be included to represent our working time in the industry.

Maple Leaf is the symbol of Gold Bond's brand which represents our Canadian pride and our support for the local economy.

STYLE GUIDE:

The style guide was developed to set standards for the writing, formatting, and design of documents.

All the requirements and standards for our brand are described and explained in our style guide.


LABEL & PACKAGE:

The label is the information display about our product showing legal information but also providing information about our product to the final users in Gold Bond we detailed about the usage of Canadian products with praible barley without preservants and our alcoholic strength.

The packaging is designated for a six-pack of our beer which includes the label information with design details to incite the user to buy our product, we also developed the package outline for printing purposes.


UX & UI DESIGN:

The UX & UI design was developed through a creative process starting with a mockup, followed by a low fidelity wireframe with more details of how content will be distributed, and finally a high fidelity wireframe that shows the final design with its content.

MOBILE LAYOUT

The mobile layout adapts to small screens providing a better experience to mobile users.


WEB LAYOUT

The web layout adapts to large screens providing a better experience to laptops, desktops and tv users.


VIDEO CAMPAIGN:

The video campaign was developed to incite and promote our products through visual advertisement, engaging our target audience.

The video includes details that represent our branding using Canadian mountains representing our Canadian pride and explains our experience in the brewery area since 1908.
